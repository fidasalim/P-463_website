M87

[5]The Virgo cluster's nucleus is home to the massive elliptical galaxy M 87. The non-thermal jet and the activity in the nucleus are what give it its notoriety. As determined by spectroscopy of whirling disc material within \(3.5 pc\) of the black hole, the black hole's mass is \(3\times 10^{8} M_{\odot}\) . VLBI imaging of its jet affords a very detailed look at what lies inside the core region( \(R < 10^{6}r_{g}\) ). The observation obtained are as follows,
(i) an asymmetric jet and counter-jet are found in the inner /(0.12 pc/).
(ii) both jet and counter-jet are edge brightened.
(iii) both jet and counter-jet show initial rapid widening.
(iv) the jet is subsequently collimated
(v) polarisation observations suggest a helical magnetic field close to the core.
The jet of M87 is observed to be accelerating smoothly and with a parabolic profile over a deprojected radius of around \(300 pc\) in high-frequency, high-resolution VLBI is studied.
